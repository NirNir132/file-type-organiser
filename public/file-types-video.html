.mp4 - MPEG-4 Part 14

The universal video format

MP4 (MPEG-4 Part 14) is the most widely used video container format, developed by the Moving Picture Experts Group. It efficiently stores video, audio, subtitles, and metadata in a single file, making it the standard for streaming, mobile devices, and web video.

üéØ Cool Facts

  • Based on Apple's QuickTime File Format but is an open standard
  • Can contain multiple video and audio tracks in different languages
  • Supports chapters, subtitles, and metadata
  • Used by virtually all streaming platforms including YouTube and Netflix

‚ú® Benefits

  • Universal compatibility across all devices and platforms
  • Excellent compression efficiency
  • Supports streaming and progressive download
  • Perfect balance of quality and file size

.avi - Audio Video Interleave

Microsoft's classic video format

AVI (Audio Video Interleave) was introduced by Microsoft in 1992 as part of its Video for Windows technology. Despite being over 30 years old, AVI remains popular for its simplicity and wide codec support, though it lacks some modern features.

üéØ Cool Facts

  • One of the oldest video container formats still in use
  • Originally designed for Windows but now cross-platform
  • Can store video without compression (uncompressed AVI)
  • Has a 2GB file size limit in the original specification

‚ú® Benefits

  • Simple container format with wide codec support
  • Good for video editing and intermediate files
  • Reliable playback on older systems
  • Less complex than modern container formats

.mov - QuickTime Movie

Apple's professional video format

MOV is a multimedia container format developed by Apple for its QuickTime framework. Known for high quality and professional features, MOV files are widely used in video production and are the foundation for the MP4 standard.

üéØ Cool Facts

  • Developed by Apple in 1991 for QuickTime
  • Became the basis for the MPEG-4 Part 14 (MP4) standard
  • Supports advanced features like alpha channels and 360¬∞ video
  • Preferred format for professional video editing on Mac

  • Excellent quality preservation
  • Advanced metadata and chapter support
  • Native optimization for Apple ecosystems
  • Professional editing features

.mkv - Matroska Video

The flexible open-source container

MKV (Matroska Video) is an open-source multimedia container that can hold virtually unlimited video, audio, picture, and subtitle tracks in one file. Named after Russian nesting dolls, it's designed to be future-proof and highly flexible.

üéØ Cool Facts

  • Named after Russian Matryoshka nesting dolls
  • Can contain unlimited tracks of video, audio, and subtitles
  • Completely open-source with no licensing fees
  • Supports chapter menus and complex metadata

‚ú® Benefits

  • Ultimate flexibility for multimedia content
  • Perfect for movies with multiple audio tracks and subtitles
  • No licensing restrictions
  • Future-proof design with extensibility

.webm - WebM Video Format

Google's web-optimized format

WebM is an open, royalty-free media format developed by Google specifically for the web. Based on the Matroska container, it uses VP8/VP9 video codecs and Vorbis/Opus audio codecs to deliver high-quality video with excellent compression.

üéØ Cool Facts

  • Developed by Google and released in 2010
  • Specifically designed for HTML5 video streaming
  • Completely royalty-free and open source
  • Supported natively by all major browsers

‚ú® Benefits

  • Optimized for web streaming and HTML5
  • No licensing fees or patent issues
  • Excellent compression for web delivery
  • Native browser support

When to Use Each Video Format

üåê For Web & Streaming

Use MP4 for maximum compatibility across all devices and platforms. For modern browsers, WebM offers excellent compression and quality.

üì± For Mobile Devices

Use MP4 with H.264 codec for universal mobile compatibility. Keep bitrates reasonable for data usage and battery life considerations.

üé¨ For Professional Editing

Use MOV on Mac systems or AVI for intermediate files. These formats preserve quality through multiple editing cycles.

üé≠ For Movie Collections

üì∫ For TV & Set-top Boxes

Video Codec vs Container

Understanding the Difference

It's important to understand that the file extension (.mp4, .avi, etc.) refers to the container format, while the actual video compression is determined by the codec:

  • Container: The "box" that holds video, audio, and metadata
  • Codec: The compression algorithm that determines quality and file size

Popular Video Codecs

  • H.264 (AVC): Most widely supported, excellent quality/size ratio
  • H.265 (HEVC): Better compression than H.264, newer standard
  • VP9: Google's codec, royalty-free, used in WebM
  • AV1: Next-generation codec, excellent compression, royalty-free

Quality vs File Size Guidelines

Resolution Recommendations

  • 720p (HD): Good for web content, smaller file sizes
  • 1080p (Full HD): Standard for most content, best quality/size balance
  • 4K (UHD): Premium quality, large file sizes, future-proofing

Bitrate Guidelines

  • 720p: 2-5 Mbps for good quality
  • 1080p: 5-10 Mbps for good quality
  • 4K: 15-25 Mbps for good quality
